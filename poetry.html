Catherine Haven Fairclough

Poetry




Creativity

In the bank of dreams
peacock feather eyes
watch sleeping minds
deposit paper promise to think
them up, withdraw
  the shadows
of thoughts. Ghosts to haunt
waking hours. The movement
behind ideas.

Neverland

Once on a time my father slipped under the sea.

He came home with pockets full of sand dollars
and tales of dancing nudibranchs
moving shell-less around their sea rose egg cluster
he would have brought back if he had more time.

As he talked the smell of brine cloaked us
covering everything -- a stampede of currents
migrated across the Earth while he crawled below
hurt by jealous coral polyps and moody rocks.

He had been searching for something older than us
a twin to the birth of the sea, a place of foiltime
where movement becomes a shadow of stillness
and never is forever.

He came back defeated
and left his pile of sand dollars
on our kitchen table made of driftwood stained
dark   except for the light of a single blue candle.

Natural History

And you think of homes
made in whale skeletons
where all the residents
are as tiny as insects
deadly as a black widows
who travel through dim
timespans to look at
empty things.
A mummy with an excess
of absence. The dread of dead
animals stuffed with anticipation
forever lunging towards extinction
the fate of 99% of all species
who ever lived   the megalodon
jaws opening to an ocean
long since changed. Dinosaur skeletons
that used to walk where we do
and if they did
maybe they too would wonder
at the scale of things.

Advice to a Daughter

Try hard to grow
until you splinter
inside an old wooden box
yellow paint flaking off
so that fingers return
with your best pieces
like bees covered in pollen.
Lock the rest away
with flower-smiles and thorn-teeth.

Remember to be a Queen
in this world. You must stay strong
Hide
your dusty tears inside
this box of hot storms
that would destroy
the hive, the flowers, you.

There is no room
for storms in summer.

Soil Types

You can't understand me
you're sand and I'm clay
their words may fall through you
like water. But for me
the water is caught and I
turn dark
and moldable.
Eventually hardening into something
you won't like
not anymore.

The Fifth Bride of Henry

The young Catherine lost
her mother -- a tragedy
that burned her
but she bandaged herself
with pretty things
animals and dance and music
(and her music teacher).
She hit court
like an asteroid.

Marriage seemed expected at that point--
middle of the summer heat and simplicity
turned Catherine into a June Queen,
but it was a long summer
and she was easily distracted
with beautiful people
hot spells.

An affair is only an affair if a woman has it
and Catherine was exiled.
But that didn't mean her last performance
couldn't be brilliant.

She rehearsed laying her head on the block
as if she was to go to sleep --
Neck relaxed, hands knitted together
and ankles crossed like a lady.
Her eyes would flutter closed
at the last moment
before the curtain came down.
Dying could be peacefully
with some practice.

Love and War

She is warmed
by the thought that somewhere
a fire burns contained
in a fireplace with a secret history
no one remembers. About
a romance that was ended when
war came and love got gangrene.

Wrapped in her red blanket
she sits reading her leather bound book
with the TV turned off. From the violence
of those black and white soldiers who know
little about love and literature.
Books see people much better
than people see books.

Her husband sitting close
their socked feet touching.
In the time where college was
the shield against war
and he could sit with her
instead of in a bunker
somewhere he could leave her alone
at any moment.

The Passenger

He'd thought his heart sank
that's true,
but really it was death
by disintegration
as he lay tangled
in the metallic spine
of this great ship --
heavily praised titan
descendent of Earth and Sky
claiming the ocean
was as easy to sail
as falling into a dog eared book.

But the journey was cut
by a decisive slice
on the weak hull --
subpar rivets,
sleeping captains,
lost binoculars and years.
Ears full with water
deaf
to the tells
of ancient hero hubris
he sinks

into a pit of anonymity
colored by lips painted
blue   skin becomes him
matches his eyes
looking towards the titanic
iceberg that could have saved them
as it killed them,
temporary refuge
cold fighting cold.

But titans are destined
to fall and take an era
with them.

A Bedtime Poem I:

Peace my child,
float away
touch the blue sky-quilt
stained where silent dreams
hide in pinpricks
of light.

Fill yourself
with galaxies
of thoughts, never leaving
you to loneliness.

Peace my child,
come down
from above
sink to your bay of a bed
drop anchor in the deep
and sleep.
